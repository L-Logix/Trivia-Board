About the Project

Everything you need to know about the Trivia Broadcast Engine — the story, the technology, and the community behind it.

Our Story

The Trivia Broadcast Engine was built for one reason: to make professional-grade live trivia accessible to everyone. Hosts, educators, event organizers, and streamers deserve a tool that works as hard as they do — without expensive licensing or closed ecosystems.

Born from a passion for game shows and a frustration with clunky alternatives, this project combines a real-time WebSocket engine, a scriptable CLI setup, and a dual-screen broadcast architecture into one cohesive package. Whether you're running a pub quiz, a classroom review session, or a Twitch stream, the Trivia Broadcast Engine gives you total control over your game.

Every component is open source, fully documented, and built to be extended. We believe great trivia software should be free — and that's exactly what this is.

Data Collection Notice

Trivia Broadcast Engine collects anonymous usage statistics to help improve the software.

What is collected:

  • Total in-game points earned and lost
  • Games played, completed, and average scores
  • Aggregate correct/incorrect answer counts
  • Streaks, buzzes, times-ups, and bonus clue stats
  • CLI and data import counts

No personal data is collected. No category names, clues, answers, player names, IP addresses, or any identifiable information is ever recorded or transmitted.

These counters are stored locally and served from your trivia server. No data is sent to external services.
